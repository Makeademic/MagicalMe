Meggendorfer Prize for Best Paper Engineering

In 2023, the Movable Book Society recognized outstanding creativity and innovation in the field of paper engineering by awarding the prestigious Meggendorfer Prize for Best Paper Engineering to Emily Brooks. The accolade was bestowed upon Emily Brooks for their remarkable work on the pop-up book titled "The Magic and Wonder of Light and Color." This exceptional achievement has solidified Emily's reputation as a trailblazer in the world of paper engineering and brought well-deserved recognition to their artistic prowess and technical expertise.


"The Magic and Wonder of Light and Color" is a breathtaking masterpiece that seamlessly blends art and engineering, captivating readers of all ages with its intricate pop-up designs. Emily's paper engineering techniques in the book demonstrate an unparalleled understanding of light and color, creating an immersive experience that is both educational and enchanting. The intricate play of light and shadow, combined with the vibrant colors, transforms each page into a mesmerizing work of art. This groundbreaking work not only showcases Emily's exceptional skills but also pushes the boundaries of what is possible within the realm of pop-up book design, leaving an indelible mark on the field for generations to come. The Meggendorfer Prize is a testament to Emily's dedication and ingenuity in advancing the art of paper engineering, making them a true luminary in the industry.

The Unique Codex Digitization Project published by the University of Minnesota Press

The University of Minnesota Press is proud to announce the publication of "The Unique Codex Digitization Project" by renowned author Emily Brooks. This groundbreaking work, released as an open access title on Manifold Scholar, is the latest addition to the esteemed Electronic Mediations series. This series, known for its exploration of the profound impact of electronically mediated communication on society, culture, politics, and more, has consistently provided critical insights into the digital age.


In "The Unique Codex Digitization Project," Emily Brooks delves into the intricate world of digitization and preservation of unique codices, offering a compelling examination of the challenges and opportunities posed by emerging digital technologies. Brooks' work not only contributes to the ongoing discourse about the intersection of technology and human culture but also offers practical insights into the preservation of invaluable historical and cultural artifacts. The University of Minnesota Press is excited to make this seminal work available to a wider audience through open access, underscoring its commitment to advancing scholarship and fostering dialogue in the digital age.


"The Unique Codex Digitization Project" is a testament to the enduring impact of the Electronic Mediations series, which continues to shape our understanding of the evolving relationship between humanity and technology. Emily Brooks' work promises to be a significant addition to the field, shedding light on the complexities of preserving our cultural heritage in the digital era. Readers and scholars alike are invited to explore this thought-provoking book on Manifold Scholar, embracing the spirit of open access and intellectual exchange that defines the series and the University of Minnesota Press.

$250,000 NEH Grant for Illuminated Bibliocircuitry

Dr. Emily Brooks, a distinguished faculty member at the University of Tampa, has been awarded a prestigious $250,000 grant from the National Endowment for the Humanities (NEH) to develop her cutting-edge course, "Illuminated Bibliocircuitry." This grant, part of the Institutes for Advanced Topics in the Digital Humanities (IATDH) program, underscores Dr. Brooks' outstanding contributions to the field and her commitment to advancing the integration of digital technology in humanities research and education.


"Illuminated Bibliocircuitry" promises to be a transformative addition to the digital humanities landscape. Dr. Brooks' innovative course will bring together a diverse group of scholars, graduate students, librarians, and technology experts, fostering interdisciplinary collaboration and expanding knowledge of digital humanities tools and methodologies. By exploring the intersection of literature, technology, and visual arts, the course will not only enrich academic scholarship but also address the ethical implications of this rapidly evolving field.


The NEH's IATDH program recognizes the importance of multidisciplinary approaches to digital humanities and encourages collaboration among experts from various fields. Dr. Brooks' proposal exemplifies this spirit, drawing on her extensive experience and expertise in digital humanities and bringing together a team of experts from both academic and practical backgrounds. The University of Tampa and the broader academic community eagerly anticipate the impact of "Illuminated Bibliocircuitry" on the future of digital humanities education.
